03 — Strategy Modules

Three player states, three different plays.

Every game-end event lands in exactly one of three buckets, defined by wallet balance and loss size relative to configurable thresholds.

Module A

Healthy wallet, high propensity

Wallet balance sits above threshold for both segments. Since these players usually convert on their own, we wait before intervening — then check wallet and pending-redeem status, and offer a bonus sized to their typical purchase, only if it's actually needed.

Condition: wallet balance above threshold
Module B

Thin wallet, heavy losses

Wallet balance is low and losses are high — the moment most likely to lead to a walk-away. After a short wait, if no redemption is pending, we send a bonus sized to the player's typical packet. If a redemption is pending, we address that first.

Condition: wallet balance and loss both past threshold
Module C

Lower balance, lower loss

Smaller stakes, lower urgency. The propensity model still classifies these events, and only the players worth nudging get a lighter-touch, selectively-targeted offer.

Condition: wallet balance and loss both below threshold
04 — What the Logic Needs

Six inputs the automation runs on.

Purchase propensity score, refreshed regularly

Real-time wallet balance

Trailing wager amount, last 30 days

Pending redemption check

Player's historical purchase packet size

Configurable wait time post game-end, plus 24-hour journey suppression so one bonus doesn't overlap another

05 — What I Flagged

Where this logic can break.

Since the trigger fires on every game-end event, one player can qualify for multiple modules in quick succession.

Without proper suppression, a player could be targeted with multiple bonus offers in a short window.

Wait times and buffer periods need careful tuning, or journeys start to overlap and cannibalize each other.
