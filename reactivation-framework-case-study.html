01 — Objective

Two goals, one automation layer.

Optimize for efficiency — move the framework onto a fully automated pipeline, cutting manual effort and shortening the gap between a player going inactive and being targeted.

Maximize growth & revenue — close the gaps the manual version couldn't reach, and let data decide where reactivation spend actually earns a return.

02 — Who This Targets

Lapsed, eligible, and worth winning back.

A player enters the framework once they've gone quiet — and clear eligibility rules keep the funnel from wasting effort on players who were never going to convert.

No play or purchase in 30+ days Converted more than 30 days ago Not deleted or self-excluded Positive lifetime revenue
03 — Scoring & Segmentation

Three signals decide the offer, not one.

Every inactive player is scored, then placed by how long they've been gone, how hard to push, and whether their wallet already has enough to reactivate on its own.

04 — What Feeds the Score

Four categories of signal.

Lifetime value — total rake, purchases, redemptions, retention spend Recent behavior — last 30/60/90-day activity & spend Peak tier reached — highest value tier & VIP status ever held Time signals — days since last play, days since conversion
05 — How a Reward Gets Picked

One illustrative example.

A high-value player, inactive 90+ days, scores into a high-pressure segment with a wallet balance already above the reactivation threshold.

Segment: high value, long-lapsed Pressure: high Wallet: above threshold

This player gets a purchase bonus only — never a gift voucher — because they already have enough balance to come back on their own. The voucher goes to players whose wallet is empty and needs seeding first.

06 — What I Found

Where the manual version was losing time.

Scoring cadence. Refresh ran on a 30-day cycle (15 days for high-value players) — a window that could be tightened for faster reactivation.

Targeting speed. Segment computation took several hours per cycle, with further delay re-targeting players missed on the first pass.

Operational load. Manual offer mapping capped segmentation at a few dozen cohorts — each refresh meant re-creating and re-mapping every offer code by hand.

Communication realignment. Messaging across channels had to be rebuilt at every refresh to stay relevant.

07 — What I Proposed

Four pillars of automation.

Computation

Automate the refresh

Move scoring and segmentation updates off manual runs and onto an automated pipeline, cutting the time between inactivity and targeting.

Offer generation

One dynamic template

Replace dozens of manually-created offer codes with a single templated voucher and bonus, dynamically valued and auto-mapped each cycle.

Communication

Event-driven tracking

Let targeting, opt-ins, and expirations update automatically wherever an offer is issued, instead of manual channel remapping.

Monitoring & error handling. A dashboard tracking offer distribution and effectiveness, plus logs for targeting errors and expiration anomalies — so issues surface before they compound.
