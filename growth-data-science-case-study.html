01 — My Role

The bridge between a marketing question and a trained model.

A data science team can build almost anything — the hard part is deciding exactly what to predict, for whom, and what to do with the answer. That's the part I owned on each of these four builds.

Frame the problem. Turn a vague growth question ("who's about to leave money on the table?") into a precise target variable a model can be trained on.

Scope the segment & trigger. Decide which players and which moment — conversion, game-end, a rolling window — the model should actually run on.

Read the model back. Interpret variable importance and performance metrics, and translate them into a targeting rule a campaign can use.

Make the spend call. Run the cost-benefit math and decide whether — and how — the model's output turns into a live campaign.

02 — The Four Models

Same partnership, four different questions.

Each model answers a different moment in the player lifecycle — but the collaboration pattern was identical every time.

Bonus Affinity

Who actually uses a bonus when offered?

Scores each player's inclination to claim a targeted bonus, using purchase and claim behavior over a rolling window. Segments players into No / Low / Medium / High affinity so spend stops flowing to players who won't use the offer.

My contribution: defined the affinity segments and the spend-optimization use case for the score.
Early VIP Model

Who's about to become a high-value player?

Predicts, within days of conversion, which new players are trending toward high-value status — paired with an early-churn model for the same window, so onboarding treatment can split high-potential from high-risk players fast.

My contribution: defined the target variable, the early-days window, and the success metrics tied to onboarding.
Purchase Prediction

Will this player buy right after this game?

At every game-end event, predicts whether a player's next action will be a purchase — so the moment can be used to either upsell confidently or offer a bonus instead of leaving it to chance.

My contribution: defined the trigger point, the priority segments, and the two resulting use cases (upsell vs. bonus).
Redeem Prediction

Is this player about to cash out?

Predicts, at game-end, whether a player is likely to request a withdrawal in the next day — creating a window to act before the redeem request, not after.

My contribution: defined the target window, the qualifying segment, and the proactive-retention use case.
