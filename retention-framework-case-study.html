01 — Problem & Objective

Spend with precision, not just intent.

The platform's retention spend was driven largely by calendar and occasion — weekends, paydays, month-end — rather than by what each customer had actually earned in return. I set out to turn historical behaviour and experimentation into targeted, accountable spend, closing the loop between customer insight and budget allocation.

  • 1

    Deliver efficiency

    Channelise spend to achieve growth at optimal pressure — not every rupee needs to be spent to earn a return.

  • 2

    Deliver growth

    Maximise revenue through deliberate, targeted increases in spend precisely where they compound.

02 — Current Focus

Three levers, one player journey.

Retention spend sustains the business across three moments in a player's lifecycle — while opening the door to new growth.

Money-in

Purchases

Campaigns that drive deposits
  1. Payday & weekend purchase bonuses
  2. Lifecycle & segmentation-based campaigns
  3. Regulatory-linked cashback, delivered as bonus
Engagement

Play & Login

Campaigns that drive activity
  1. Gameplay — challenges, missions, leaderboards, streak rewards
  2. Logins — vouchers, tournaments, subscription passes
  3. Bet upsell / cross-sell via subscription passes
Money-out

Withdrawals

Campaigns shaped by redeem intent
  1. Reactive — smart offers triggered at the moment of redeem
  2. Proactive — targeting before a redeem decision is made
  3. Pass-linked offers after a game session ends

04 — What I Found

What a calendar-driven model misses.

The existing strategy delivered on business outcomes, but not with the precision to make every rupee count — and it occasionally clashed with other value systems already in place.

1

Spend reaches customers who were never going to yield the outcome it was meant to drive.

2

Additional spend lands on customers where no incremental return was ever likely.

3

Growth opportunities across the base go unidentified — or identified too late to capitalise on.

05 — My Approach

Introducing Engagement Value (EV).

The platform already ran two reward programmes: a Loyalty track measured over the mid-term via wager milestones, and a Retention track measured monthly and shaped by market and budget factors. Both were useful — neither quantified a customer's total relationship with the business. I designed EV to be that single number: a 1–5 score representing a customer's full relationship value, used to calibrate retention spend without over- or under-investing.

1–5
EV score range
01

Long-horizon frequency

How often the customer plays across a trailing multi-month window — recurring and reactivated play alike.

02

Monthly active days

Within a given month, how many distinct days the customer typically plays.

03

Gross revenue generated

Total revenue the customer has generated over the same trailing window.

04

Net revenue generated

Net revenue over the same horizon — the number that actually matters to the business.

05

Spend pressure

Retention spend as a percentage of gross revenue — how much it costs to keep this customer engaged.

Long horizonLoyalty

Rewards value created over the mid-term relationship. Runs near-uniformly for every registered customer, measured chiefly against preset wager milestones.

Short horizonRetention

Rewards value created monthly or weekly. Shifts frequently with market, budget and business factors — this is the layer EV was designed to sharpen.

06 — FRAMES

Six signals, refreshed daily, that keep EV honest.

EV describes value over the long run. I built FRAMES as its short-term companion — watching the last 30 days, catching deviation early enough to intervene before it shows up in next month's score.

08 — How It Runs

Operating the framework, once live.

Update frequency

Monthly EV, daily FRAMES

EV recalculates once a month for every customer — except new customers, held back for a ramp-up period until there's enough signal to read behaviour reliably. FRAMES updates daily, tracking interventions as they land.

Budget allocation

From calendar to cohort

Budget shifts from being allocated by occasion — inactivity, upsell, weekend, month-end — to being allocated by EV cohort.

  • Right pressure, held at the customer level
  • Growth & efficiency targets set per EV cohort
  • Programmes mapped to objective via EV — who to upsell, and when
  • Net revenue kept in check daily and weekly
09 — Rollout Plan

Eight steps from proposal to autopilot.

The build sequence I laid out once the approach had stakeholder buy-in.

Score EV across the base

Complete EV scoring for the full active-user base using trailing behavioural history.

Define FRAMES classes

Finalise the dimensions and classes within FRAMES.

Build the data model

Stand up a warehouse model that updates every value at its intended frequency.

Consume for budget & campaigns

Let EV and FRAMES actually drive spend allocation and campaign targeting.

Push classes to activation tools

Sync EV classes into the CRM/engagement stack in use for campaign execution.

Publish dashboards

Report on EV cohorts and player transitions against intervention campaigns and their impact.

Test player-level monitoring

Validate the ability to track every player's transitions, growth and efficiency.

Automate

Once tested and value is assessed, automate the retention strategy end to end.
