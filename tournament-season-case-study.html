01 — Overview

A tournament season built to do three jobs at once.

This wasn't the first run of the program — an earlier edition had already proven the format could draw a crowd. This edition had to go further: link the tournament excitement to everyday cash-game play, and use it as a lever across the entire player base, not just the top of it.

Acquisition — high-value players Engagement — mid & low-value cohorts, via play frequency Reactivation — lapsed high-value players
02 — What the Last Edition Taught Us

Five lessons that shaped this one.

Offline moments build trust fast. A grand, real-world prize event had an outsized effect on brand credibility — more than any in-app mechanic alone.

High-value acquisition barely moved. The tournament drew a crowd, but converting genuinely new high-value players from competitors proved much harder to shift — and harder still to measure.

Reactivation of long-lapsed, high-value players hit an all-time high — the single strongest result from the last edition.

Leaderboard + challenge combinations are exploitable. Without the right caps, players found arbitrage routes and the same names kept winning.

Uncapped challenge spend leaks money. Caps need to sit at the player level, not the challenge level, or the economics quietly break.

03 — Segment Strategy

Every cohort gets a different reason to play.

The same season had to motivate five very different player groups — so each got its own mechanic, tuned to what actually moves that cohort.

HVP & Ultra-HVP

Chase the grand reward

The marquee tournament and its offline finale — built to make top-tier players rake more, more often, and stay in for longer sessions.

New players

An early route to the finale

A weekly newcomer leaderboard selects rising players into the finale pool — a fast track that doesn't require months of history.

MVP & HVP

Missions, tourneys, leaderboards

Multi-day missions and freerolls give this cohort a reason to return; leaderboard chasing pushes session depth once they're in.

LVP

A week-long reward rush

Wager-based milestones spread across a full week, extending play across multiple sessions instead of one-off spikes.

New & reactivated

Build the return habit

Freerolls and login missions hook the first few days back; win missions convert that habit into real rake.

06 — Risks & Guardrails

What I flagged before launch.

Responsible gaming flags. Players chasing leaderboard rank can trigger excessive-play flags — the RG logic needed a review pass before this could go live.

Spend caps at the wrong level. Prior challenge-based mechanics leaked spend because caps sat on the challenge, not the player — a fix carried into this season's design.
